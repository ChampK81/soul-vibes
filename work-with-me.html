What is Yoga Therapy?

Yoga therapy is a therapeutic application of yoga practices designed to support physical, mental, and emotional well-being. Unlike general yoga classes, yoga therapy is individualized and paced with care, with an emphasis on nervous system regulation, self-awareness, and integration into daily life.

If you're curious about how this work might fit your needs, scroll on to explore the different ways we can work together.

How yoga therapy is different

While traditional yoga classes often follow a set structure and pace, yoga therapy adapts practices to the individual. Sessions may focus less on physical postures and more on breath, rest, gentle movement, and noticing internal experience. The goal is not performance or flexibility, but building capacity, choice, and a more responsive relationship with the body.

What yoga therapy focuses on

Yoga therapy often supports nervous system awareness, emotional regulation, and sustainable self-care. Practices are selected to meet a person where they are, with attention to pacing, consent, and personal agency. This work is commonly used alongside other forms of support, such as counselling or medical care.

Working together

All offerings are grounded in trauma-informed yoga therapy principles — personalized, accessible, and focused on supporting the nervous system rather than performance.
