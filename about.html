✦ About Kayley

Care, responsibility, and a commitment to ongoing learning.

Soul Vibes is grounded in the belief that yoga is for everyone — and that a trauma-informed, anti-oppressive approach is not optional, it is essential.

Kayley Champagne

Yoga Therapist (Practicum) · CYT-500

I am currently a practicum student working toward my certification as a Yoga Therapist, while also holding a CYT-500 certification in Trauma Informed Yoga Psychology.

I take my commitment to providing a trauma informed approach and experience very seriously. My mission with Soul Vibes is to provide a yoga experience that is anti-oppressive, anti-racist, decolonized, and rooted in social justice. This mission is guided through the use of Patanjali's 8 Limb path as it honours and respects the ancient teachings derived from South East Asia.

I truly believe in the benefits of all aspects of yoga as I have gratefully experienced many of them myself. Through this experience a deep rooted passion for sharing these teachings has taken hold and allowed me the privilege to be able to share it with others. I approach this work with care, responsibility and a commitment to ongoing learning.

With gratitude,
Kayley Champagne

A yoga experience for all bodies, all nervous systems.

Trauma informed

Every session is grounded in a trauma informed approach — prioritizing safety, choice, and autonomy above all else. You are always in control of your own experience.

Anti-oppressive & decolonized

Soul Vibes is committed to a yoga practice that is anti-oppressive, anti-racist, and rooted in social justice — honouring the ancient teachings of Patanjali's 8 Limb path.

Accessible by design

No experience, no particular body type, and no baseline wellness is required. The practice adapts to meet you exactly where you are.

Land Acknowledgement

I acknowledge my presence and express deep gratitude for living and working on the lands known as Turtle Island. I am thankful for the privilege of raising my family here, and for the opportunities for work, rest, and play this land makes possible. I honour the stewards of these lands who came long before us and recognize our shared responsibility to continue caring for this place with respect and love.

I come from both Settler and Métis ancestry and acknowledge my presence as both a visitor and a steward on Treaty 7 territory, the traditional lands of the Blackfoot Confederacy — including the Siksika, Piikani, and Kainai Nations — as well as the Tsuut’ina Nation and the Stoney Nakoda Nations of Goodstoney, Chiniki, and Bearspaw peoples. I also honour my Métis ancestors of the Métis Nation of Alberta, District 4.

I express gratitude for my teachers, my teachers’ teachers, and the roots of yoga that extend back thousands of years to South East Asia. I offer this acknowledgement with humility and a commitment to ongoing learning, care, and responsibility in how I live and work on this land.
